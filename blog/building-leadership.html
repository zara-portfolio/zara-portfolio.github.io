Building Leadership Through Service and Creativity

November 22, 2025

This year, I have taken on several key roles and projects that reflect my commitment to community service and student leadership. As the president of my school’s National Honor Society chapter, I coordinated a range of initiatives designed to support local needs and strengthen school involvement.

Our chapter successfully carried out two food drives, a clothing drive, and a pet-supply drive. In addition, I helped organize movie nights and bake sales that served both as community events and fundraising opportunities. One of our most notable achievements was raising approximately $320 for a local clinic, demonstrating what a small school can accomplish with focused effort.

Beyond NHS, I represent my grade as a class representative on student council, where I contribute to discussions, planning, and decision-making for school activities.

In parallel with these responsibilities, I am preparing a submission for the 2026 New York Times writing competition. My topic explores why acting should be included in school curricula, highlighting its benefits for confidence, communication, and creativity.

This year’s work has given me experience in managing projects, collaborating with peers, and balancing leadership with academic and creative commitments.
